Hi! We are Penn FLiTE

A community for First-generation, Low-income (FGLI) students in Technology and Engineering

About Us

Who We Are

FLiTE is a student organization that provides catered support, opportunities and development for First-generation, Low-income (FGLI) students at Penn passionate about technology and engineering to ensure success for a population with unique needs.


Our Mission

We believe in fostering equal opportunity and support to help all students of all backgrounds fulfill their passions and overcome all barriers. We plan on fulfilling our mission by providing resouces, mentorship, and a community for FGLI students in engineering

First generation low income students are equally as likely to select engineering and computer science majors upon entering college as their non-FGLI counterparts.

First generation low income students however are more than twice likely to drop out of engineering and computer science, indicating a need for a support space.

Yet, those that stay stick with their original computer science/engineering majors even more than their non-FGLI peers, signaling a desire and persistence in the subject.

Mentorship

We aim to provide academic, professional, and general guidance for incoming students through our mentorship program that pairs upperclassmen with freshmen.

Resources

We plan to partner with a variety organizations from technology companies to community non-profits to provide a wide-degree of academic and professional resources.

Collaboration

We hope to develop our students through interdisciplinary collaboration to create leading engineers that will create innovative solutions to a variety of society's problems.
